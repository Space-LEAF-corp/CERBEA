# CERBEA Specification v1.0

## Executive Summary

CERBEA (Cooperative Energy Recycling & Bio-Engineering Advanced Architecture) is a comprehensive system for advanced energy recovery, conversion, and distribution. This specification outlines the engineering requirements, module interactions, and safety protocols.

## System Overview

### Core Objectives

1. **Maximum Energy Recovery** - Capture energy from all available sources
2. **Cooperative Distribution** - Intelligent, redundant grid management
3. **Safety First** - Multi-layered safety protocols with human oversight
4. **Ethical Stewardship** - Transparent monitoring and child safety

### High-Level Architecture

```
BER Core (Reactor) → TKRS (Recovery) → DEN Grid → End Users
                  ↓
            GEO Oversight (Ledger, Safety, Ethics)
                  ↑
    MEN (Materials) ← BESL (Bio) ← User Feedback
```

## Module Specifications

### BER - Binding Energy Reactor

**Purpose**: Advanced energy generation through controlled plasma and fusion processes

**Key Components**:
- Plasma confinement using triple-redundant magnetic fields
- Fusion pathway optimization
- Mass-defect energy extraction
- Thermal interface with TKRS

**Safety Features**:
- Triple-redundant containment layers
- Automatic shutdown protocol
- Real-time confinement monitoring

### TKRS - Thermal-Kinetic Recovery System

**Purpose**: Multi-modal energy recovery from thermal and kinetic waste

**Recovery Pathways**:
- Thermoelectric (TEG arrays)
- Stirling cycle engines
- Piezoelectric floor systems
- Flywheel inertial capture
- Atmospheric biofilter processing

### DEN - Distributed Energy Network

**Purpose**: Cooperative, fault-tolerant energy distribution

**Features**:
- Ant colony optimization for flow balancing
- Cooperative balancing algorithms
- Mesh node handshaking
- Failure recovery protocols
- Microwave and laser energy transfer

### MEN - Materials Energy Nexus

**Purpose**: Recovery and reformation of materials from debris

**Process**:
1. Grapple collection and intake
2. Thermal smelting
3. Alloy reformation
4. Material packet distribution

### BESL - Bio-Eco Sustainable Living

**Purpose**: Biological energy generation and sustainable systems

**Systems**:
- Algae photosynthesis panels
- Water electrolysis units
- Centripetal bearing harvest
- Child-safe isolation zones
- Emergency backup systems

### GEO - Governance & Ethics Oversight

**Purpose**: Ethical monitoring, ledger management, and emergency response

**Functions**:
- Risk monitoring and anomaly detection
- Child safety scanning
- Energy ledger and accounting
- Cascade shutdown protocols
- Steward beacon activation

## Integration Points

All modules communicate through the **Shared Kernel**:
- Energy packets with quality metrics
- Stewardship tags for accountability
- Capability tokens for authorization
- Module contracts for guaranteed interfaces
- Safety contracts for emergency protocols

## Safety Protocols

### Levels of Safety

1. **Operational Safety** - BER containment, pressure limits
2. **System Safety** - Grid redundancy, node isolation
3. **Environmental Safety** - TKRS cooling, emissions monitoring
4. **Child Safety** - Isolation zones, emergency backup
5. **Ethical Safety** - GEO oversight, steward review

### Emergency Response

- **Level 1**: Anomaly alert to GEO
- **Level 2**: Module isolation
- **Level 3**: Cascade shutdown sequence
- **Level 4**: Emergency beacon activation

## Performance Targets

- **Energy Efficiency**: >85% recovery across all pathways
- **Uptime**: >99.9% with graceful degradation
- **Response Time**: <100ms for critical decisions
- **Safety Latency**: <1s for emergency shutdown
- **Scalability**: Up to 10,000 DEN grid nodes

- Advanced materials from space-sourced debris
